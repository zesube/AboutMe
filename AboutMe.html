About Me

Who Am I?

Hello, my name is Zawadi Esùbe, I am 19-years-old on a journey of self-discovery and personal growth.
I am currently pursuing my associate’s degree at Ivy Tech Community College.
As I navigate this journey, I am exploring the exciting world of technology while developing my personal abilities and technical skills.

My last name can be the most difficult to pronounce, but I’ll help you: it’s E-SÙ-Be.
My last name is a passed on name meaning "a guy born in a family of many girls- orginally my grandpa's older brother's name", both names have significantly shaped my identity.
To understand my last name, we need to explore its origins.
It comes from the Democratic Republic of Congo and is spoken by the Babembe ethnic group.
To me, my last name carries deep meaning because it serves as a reminder of who I am and where I come from, culturally and in terms of my identity.

Aside from school, I spend half of my time working, while the other portion is dedicated to attending my church choir meetings.
My job is not just any job; even though it may not require professional skills that some people consider essential, it holds significant value for me.
Growing up, I was never a people person, and engaging in conversations with others always felt challenging.
However, since starting my first real job, I’ve learned how to hold conversations, which has been beneficial overall.
These interactions have taught me to appreciate other people’s perspectives and, most importantly, have helped me develop communication skills that will benefit my future career.

Apart from work, my hobbies include cooking, learning, and spending time on YouTube.
I’m not an outdoors person, but I typically use my YouTube time to learn new things.
Currently, I’m learning to code.
I focus on acquiring skills that will benefit my career or that are valuable for personal development.
Being an indoors person is not a waste of time; rather, it is an opportunity to gain new skills.
